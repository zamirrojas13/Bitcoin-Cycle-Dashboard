⚠️ Margin & Liquidation Risk
TierEntryMargin used Liq. priceBuffer to liq.Status
🕒 How Coinbase CFM margin works: Mark-to-market daily at ~4 PM ET — cash moves in/out of your balance every night. Maintenance margin = 50% of initial. Liquidation fires automatically on a wick to the mark price (BTC index), not last traded. Critical: Fri 5 PM ET → Sun 6 PM ET the market halts ~45 hours — BTC keeps moving globally and you cannot exit. A gap opening Monday against you past maintenance = instant liquidation. Historical weekend gaps: often $2K–$5K+.

📅 BIT Contract Roll Schedule
BIT contracts expire on the last Friday of each month. Roll 1–2 weeks before expiry: close current month, open next month to avoid forced settlement.
⚡ Coinbase CFM Quick Reference
⚖️ US Section 1256 (Futures Tax): Coinbase CFM BIT contracts qualify as regulated futures contracts — taxed 60% long-term / 40% short-term regardless of how long you hold. This is favorable vs spot (which uses your actual holding period). A $100K gain on futures = ~$23K federal tax (vs ~$37K at short-term rates). Consult a qualified CPA — rules vary by jurisdiction and your income level.

Execution Checklist
⚠️ No native OCO — place TP and SL as separate GTC limit orders. Cancel the other one manually when filled.
📅 Roll before expiry — close current month and open next month 1–2 weeks before settlement to avoid forced cash out.
🎯 Use limit orders at entry to pay maker fees (0.01–0.05%) not taker (0.10–0.11%). Saves ~0.1% on large positions.
📈 No funding rate decay — unlike perps, you can hold through the full bear without bleeding funding. This is why we use expiring contracts.
🔒 Risk rule — never deploy more than you can afford to lose entirely. Leverage amplifies losses too.
